The solution

Two engines, one connected view

We deploy two separate Odoo 19 instances on two virtual machines. Community carries the high-volume, many-user work for free; Enterprise carries HR (and optionally Accounting) on a tiny number of paid licences. A secure integration keeps them in sync.

Licence options

Buy 1 user, or 2? Our honest take

The bare minimum to make this work is one Enterprise user (HR only). We recommend two — adding the accountant to Enterprise — so that journal entries, double-entry bookkeeping and statutory reporting are done correctly. Odoo's Accounting is significantly more capable than Community's basic Invoicing.

Good to know

Assumptions & notes

1
Why two instances? Odoo Enterprise is licensed per user. Running everyday, many-user work on free Community and keeping only HR/Accounting on Enterprise is what keeps your licence cost at 1–2 users.
2
Community Accounting is basic. Community offers Invoicing, not the full Accounting app. For correct double-entry books and statutory reports we recommend Option B (accountant on Enterprise).
3
Custom plan is required. Self-hosting and the external-API integration are only available on Odoo's Custom plan — not the cheaper Standard (Odoo-hosted) plan.
4
The sync is near-real-time, not instant. Data flows on a scheduled interval (e.g. every few minutes). This is normal and reliable for finance reporting.
5
Hosting & licence are pass-through. Both are paid by the client directly to the cloud provider and to Odoo respectively. Centrix Plus sets them up and manages them on your behalf.
6
Scope changes. Additional apps (POS, Appointments, Marketing, etc.) or extra users can be added later and would be quoted separately.
